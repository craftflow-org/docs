## Call recording consent

CraftFlow records customer calls. **It is your responsibility** as the business to comply with the call recording consent laws in your jurisdiction.

### United States

Call recording consent laws vary by state:

| Consent type | States |
|-------------|--------|
| **One-party consent** (only one party needs to consent — e.g., the employee) | Most states, including TX, FL, NY, IL |
| **Two-party / all-party consent** (all parties must consent) | CA, CT, FL*, IL*, MD, MA, MI, MT, NV, NH, OR, PA, WA |

<Warning>
  Florida and Illinois are listed in both categories because their laws have nuances. Consult legal counsel if you operate in these states.
</Warning>

The safest practice in any state is to inform callers at the start of the call that it may be recorded (e.g., "This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes.").

### Canada

Canada follows a federal privacy law (PIPEDA) that generally requires consent before recording. Provinces may have additional requirements.

### Other countries

Consult local legal counsel for recording consent requirements in your country.
