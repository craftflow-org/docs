title: "Scorecard Best Practices"
description: "How to build scorecards that drive accurate AI scoring and meaningful coaching."
---

Scorecards are the foundation of Craft. Get them right and the AI becomes your best coaching tool. Get them wrong and scores feel random. This guide covers the 5 rules that separate great scorecards from frustrating ones.

## How Scoring Works

Each scorecard item is scored 0-5 based on what the AI can detect in the conversation:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Fully executed — nailed it |
| 4 | Mostly there — minor room to improve |
| 3 | Adequate — got the job done |
| 2 | Partial — significant room to improve |
| 1 | Barely addressed — minimally touched |
| 0 | Missed — not addressed at all |
| N/A | Not applicable to this conversation |

## Rule 1: Be Specific and Objective

The more precisely you describe the behavior, the more accurate the scoring.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Bad">
    "Did the rep present multiple options to the customer?"
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Good">
    "Did the rep present at least three options (Good, Better, Best) starting with the Best option first? Did the rep clearly state what's included in each option and the differences?"
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Rule 2: One Behavior Per Step

Keep each step focused on a single behavior. This makes scoring accurate and coaching actionable.

- **Bad:** "Present options, explain financing, and handle objections"
- **Good:** Three separate steps — Options Presentation, Financing Explanation, Objection Handling

When you combine behaviors, a rep who nails one but misses another gets an averaged score that helps no one.

## Rule 3: Focus on Business-Driving Behaviors

Ask yourself: **"Does this behavior directly impact close rate, average ticket, or customer satisfaction?"**

**High-impact (include these):**
- Options presentation
- Objection handling
- Value positioning
- Financing discussion
- Close attempt

**Low-impact (skip these):**
- Generic small talk
- Rapport building (unless specific and measurable)
- Administrative tasks

## Rule 4: Be Explicit About Timing

The AI doesn't enforce order based on step sequence. If timing matters, say so in the criteria.

- **Bad:** "Check in with customer during the middle of the conversation"
- **Good:** "Does the rep talk to the customer DURING the inspection? (after starting the inspection but before finishing it)"

## Rule 5: Use Observable Criteria

The AI can only score what it can detect in the transcript. Avoid subjective language.

| Subjective (avoid) | Objective (use instead) |
|---|---|
| "Felt consultative" | "Asked at least 3 discovery questions and paused for responses" |
| "Seemed confident" | "Used declarative statements ('This is what I recommend') rather than tentative language ('maybe', 'I think')" |
| "Natural flow" | "Transitioned from inspection to presentation by summarizing findings before showing options" |

**Test:** Ask yourself "Could two people reading the same transcript agree on whether this happened?" If not, make it more specific.

## Advanced: Weights and N/A

### Scorecard Weights

Each item has a weight (default 1) that controls how much it affects the overall score. Bump high-impact steps to weight 2-3 so they move the needle more. See [Scorecard Weights](/help/getting-started/scorecard-weights) for the full math.

### N/A Conditions

Allow the AI to mark a step as "Not Applicable" when it doesn't apply. Always specify what triggers N/A.

**Example:** "Explain membership benefits → Mark N/A if customer states they are already a member or rep references their existing membership."

<Tip>
Create separate steps with N/A conditions rather than one step with branching logic. It's cleaner and scores more accurately.
</Tip>

## Example: In-Home Sales Scorecard

| # | Step | What to Score |
|---|------|--------------|
| 1 | Greeting & rapport | Did the rep introduce themselves by name and build rapport within the first 2 minutes? |
| 2 | Discovery questions | Did the rep ask at least 3 questions about the customer's needs, timeline, and budget? |
| 3 | Inspection walkthrough | Did the rep explain what they're inspecting and what they're looking for? |
| 4 | Options presentation | Did the rep present at least 3 options (Good, Better, Best) starting with Best? |
| 5 | Financing | Did the rep proactively mention financing options before the customer asked about price? |
| 6 | Objection handling | When the customer raised concerns, did the rep acknowledge and address them? |
| 7 | Close attempt | Did the rep ask for the business with a direct close question? |

<Tip>
Start with 5-8 steps. You can always add more later. Fewer steps = more accurate scoring and clearer coaching conversations.
</Tip>
