Adoption is the #1 challenge in the first month. The companies that win with Craft get to 90%+ recording rates within 30 days. Here's how they do it.

## Frame It Right

How you introduce Craft determines whether reps buy in or resist. The wrong framing kills adoption before it starts.

| Wrong framing | Right framing |
|---|---|
| "We're going to monitor your calls" | "We're investing in helping you sell more and earn more" |
| "Corporate wants us to use this" | "This is like having a personal sales coach on every call" |
| "Everyone has to record starting Monday" | "Top performers are already using this — here's what they're seeing" |

Reps need to believe Craft helps **them**, not just management. Lead with what's in it for them: better coaching, higher close rates, bigger paychecks.

## The Proven Rollout Playbook

### Week 1: Start with volunteers (3-5 reps)

Pick your best reps or most willing volunteers. Don't start with the skeptics.

- Get them recording every appointment
- Review their recordings and leave helpful comments
- Let them experience the feedback loop

### Week 2: Build champions

- Ask early adopters to share what they've learned
- Highlight specific wins — "Jake used Checkpoint on a tough objection and closed the deal"
- Have champions talk to the broader team (peer credibility > management mandate)

### Week 3-4: Roll out company-wide

- Now you have proof, internal advocates, and refined scorecards
- Roll out to the full team with champions already in place
- Set clear expectations: every appointment gets recorded

## What Top-Performing Managers Do

The best rollouts are **manager-led, not mandated**:

- **Review recordings weekly** and leave comments — reps need to see that someone is actually using the data
- **Reference specific recordings in 1:1s** — "I listened to your appointment at the Johnson house. Here's what I noticed..."
- **Celebrate improvements publicly** — "Sarah improved her options presentation score from 2.1 to 4.3 this month"
- **Lead by example** — if you manage field reps, record a ride-along yourself

## Track and Reinforce

Monitor these metrics weekly on the dashboard:

- **Recording volume by rep** — who's recording and who isn't?
- **Recording consistency** — are reps recording every appointment or just cherry-picking good ones?

Follow up individually with reps who aren't recording. Usually it's one of three reasons:

1. **They forgot** — help them build the habit (open Craft when you arrive, hit Record before you knock)
2. **They're afraid of being judged** — reinforce that this is coaching, not surveillance
3. **Technical issue** — check permissions, app version, phone storage

<Warning>
Don't let non-adoption slide. If 3 out of 10 reps aren't recording, the other 7 will notice and start questioning why they bother. Consistency across the team matters.
</Warning>
