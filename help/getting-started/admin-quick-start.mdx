Get your team set up on Craft in 30 minutes. This guide walks you through the three things every admin needs to do first: add your team, build your scorecard, and learn your dashboard.

## Step 1: Invite Your Team (5 minutes)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings > Users" />
  <Step title="Click Add User" />
  <Step title="Enter their email and name" />
  <Step title="Choose their role">

| Role | What They Can Do | Best For |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| **Admin** | Everything — users, scorecards, billing, all recordings | Owners, ops managers |
| **Manager** | View team recordings, add coaching comments, override scores | Team leads, supervisors |
| **Rep** | Record, view their own recordings and scores | Field reps, techs, CSRs |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign them to a team">
    E.g., "North Region", "Call Center", "Inside Sales".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Send Invite">
    They'll get a setup email.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Start with a small pilot group (3-5 reps) before rolling out company-wide. This lets you refine your scorecard and build internal champions.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Build Your First Scorecard (15 minutes)

Your scorecard is the backbone of Craft. It defines what a great sales conversation looks like, and the AI scores every interaction against it.

### How Scoring Works

Each scorecard step is scored 0-5:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Nailed it — executed completely and effectively |
| 4 | Mostly there — minor room for improvement |
| 3 | Adequate — got the job done |
| 2 | Partial — significant room for improvement |
| 1 | Barely touched — minimally addressed |
| 0 | Missed it — not addressed at all |
| N/A | Didn't apply to this conversation |

### Five Rules for a Good Scorecard

1. **Be specific.** "Did the rep present at least three options (Good, Better, Best) starting with the Best option first?" beats "Did the rep present options?"
2. **One behavior per step.** Don't combine "present options, explain financing, and handle objections" into one step. Split them up.
3. **Focus on what drives revenue.** Options presentation, objection handling, value positioning, closing technique. Skip low-impact steps like small talk.
4. **Be explicit about timing.** If order matters, say so: "Does the rep talk to the customer DURING the inspection (after starting but before finishing)?"
5. **Use observable criteria.** The AI scores what it can hear. "Asked at least 3 discovery questions" works. "Felt consultative" doesn't.

### Example: In-Home Sales Scorecard

| Step | What to Score |
|------|--------------|
| 1. Greeting & rapport | Did the rep introduce themselves by name and build rapport within the first 2 minutes? |
| 2. Discovery questions | Did the rep ask at least 3 questions about the customer's needs, timeline, and budget? |
| 3. Inspection walkthrough | Did the rep explain what they're inspecting and what they're looking for? |
| 4. Options presentation | Did the rep present at least 3 options (Good, Better, Best) starting with Best? |
| 5. Financing | Did the rep proactively mention financing options before the customer asked about price? |
| 6. Objection handling | When the customer raised concerns, did the rep acknowledge and address them? |
| 7. Close attempt | Did the rep ask for the business with a direct close question? |

<Tip>
Start with 5-8 steps. You can always add more later. Fewer steps = more accurate scoring and clearer coaching.
</Tip>

## Step 3: Learn Your Dashboard (10 minutes)

### The Heatmap

The heatmap shows average scores by step and by rep. It's the fastest way to spot coaching opportunities.

- **Red cells** = low scores (coaching needed)
- **Green cells** = high scores (doing well)
- **One step red across the whole team?** That's a training issue — the team needs help on that step
- **One rep red across all steps?** That's an individual coaching issue — they need 1:1 attention

### Key Metrics to Watch

- **Overall score trends** — Is the team improving week over week?
- **Recording volume** — Are reps actually recording? (adoption is the #1 early challenge)
- **Top and bottom performers** — Who needs coaching? Who can mentor others?

### Suggested Weekly Routine

- Review **2-3 recordings per rep per week** (mix of high and low scores)
- Add **coaching comments** on specific moments (reps see these in their app)
- Use your **1:1s** to discuss feedback and set goals
- Check the heatmap every Monday to plan the week's coaching focus

## Common First-Week Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why do scores seem off at first?">
    Your scorecard might need tuning. If a step is consistently scored too high or too low, make the criteria more specific. Scoring accuracy improves as you refine your scorecard language.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My team is resistant to being recorded.">
    This is normal. Position Craft as a coaching investment, not surveillance. Share it as "we're investing in helping you sell more and earn more." Start with volunteers or top performers who'll become advocates.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recordings aren't linking to the right customer/job.">
    This usually means the CRM integration needs adjustment. Check that your ServiceTitan (or other CRM) tenant ID is connected correctly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I have different scorecards for different teams?">
    Yes. Many companies use one scorecard for in-home sales, another for call center, and another for inside sales. Each team sees only their relevant scorecard.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What's Next?

1. **Week 1:** Focus on adoption — make sure every rep is recording every appointment
2. **Week 2:** Refine your scorecard based on the first batch of scores
3. **Week 3-4:** Establish your coaching rhythm (review, comment, discuss in 1:1s)
4. **Month 2+:** Start tracking score trends and correlating process adherence with revenue
