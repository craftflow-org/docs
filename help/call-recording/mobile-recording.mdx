---
title: "Recording Calls on Mobile"
description: "How technicians use the CraftFlow mobile app to record field calls."
---

Technicians record customer calls directly from the CraftFlow mobile app. The app handles uploading and processing automatically once the recording is done.

## Before you start

- Download the **CraftFlow** app from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
- Sign in with your CraftFlow credentials — your manager will have sent you an invite
- Make sure you have a stable internet connection, or enable offline mode (see below)

<Warning>
  Always inform customers that the call is being recorded. Check your local recording consent laws — some states require two-party consent.
</Warning>

## Recording a call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the CraftFlow app">
    Tap the CraftFlow icon on your home screen and log in if prompted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new recording">
    Tap the **Record** button on the home screen. You can optionally add a note about the job (customer name, job type) before starting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record your call">
    Hold the phone normally during the call. Keep the mic unobstructed. The app will display a timer showing the recording length.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop and upload">
    Tap **Stop** when the call ends. The app will automatically upload the recording. You'll see a confirmation once it's queued.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Offline recording

If you're in an area with poor signal, CraftFlow will save the recording locally and upload it automatically when you reconnect to the internet.

<Note>
  Offline recordings are stored on your device until uploaded. Don't uninstall the app or delete the app's data before the upload completes.
</Note>

## Viewing your own recordings

After a recording is processed (usually within a few minutes), you can view your transcript and feedback directly in the app under **My Recordings**.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The upload is stuck">
    Check your internet connection. If you're on Wi-Fi, try switching to cellular. The upload will resume automatically once connectivity is restored.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The recording quality is poor">
    Make sure the phone's microphone isn't covered. Avoid recording in high-noise environments like running trucks or outdoor equipment. The built-in mic works well for most calls.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I can't log in">
    Make sure you're using the email address your manager used to invite you. If you've forgotten your password, tap **Forgot password** on the login screen.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
