CraftFlow turns call analysis into actionable coaching. Instead of vague performance reviews, managers can point to specific moments in real calls and assign structured coaching tasks.

## Coaching assignments

A coaching assignment is a task you assign to a CSR or technician based on something you found in a call. It includes:

- A description of what to work on
- A link to the specific call (and timestamp) for reference
- A due date
- A completion status

To create one, open any call or recording and click **Assign Coaching**.

## Coaching workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review flagged calls">
    Start with calls that scored below your threshold or were manually flagged. Go to **Call Center AI → Calls** and filter for flagged calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Listen to the moment that needs work">
    Use the transcript to jump to the relevant part of the call. Read or listen to what happened.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a coaching assignment">
    Click **Assign Coaching**, add your note explaining what the CSR should do differently, and set a due date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="CSR completes the assignment">
    The CSR sees their pending coaching in the app. Once they've reviewed and applied the feedback, they mark it complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track completion">
    Managers can see all open and completed coaching assignments under **AI Ridealong → Coaching**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Briefings

Briefings are short pre-shift summaries you can create for your team. They're useful for reinforcing a specific skill across the whole team based on patterns you're seeing in the call data.

Go to **AI Ridealong → Briefings** to create one.

## Coaching best practices

<Tip>
  Keep coaching assignments focused on one thing at a time. A CSR told to improve their greeting, their closing, and their urgency framing all at once won't improve any of them.
</Tip>

- **Pair coaching with examples** — always link to the specific call moment so the CSR can hear what you're referring to
- **Acknowledge wins** — use coaching assignments for positive reinforcement too, not just corrections
- **Follow up** — check whether the behavior changed in subsequent calls a week later
