---
title: "Scorecards"
description: "Build custom scorecards to automatically grade every call your CSRs take."
---

Scorecards are the criteria CraftFlow uses to evaluate calls. You define what a great call looks like, and the AI scores every call against those criteria automatically.

## Creating a scorecard

Go to **Call Center AI → Scorecards** and click **New Scorecard**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name your scorecard">
    Give it a clear name, like "Inbound Booking Call" or "After-Hours Call." You can have multiple scorecards for different call types.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add sections">
    Organize your scorecard into sections, such as "Greeting," "Needs Assessment," "Closing," and "Booking." Sections help structure the scoring and make feedback easier to understand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add items to each section">
    Each item is a specific thing the CSR should do or say. Examples:
    - Did the CSR greet the customer by name?
    - Did the CSR ask about the urgency of the issue?
    - Did the CSR confirm the appointment date and time?

    Set each item as **pass/fail** or assign it a point value for weighted scoring.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your pass threshold">
    Choose the minimum score or pass rate required for a call to be considered passing. Calls below this threshold will be flagged for review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and activate">
    Once activated, the scorecard will be applied to all new calls automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Scorecard tips

<Tip>
  Start simple. A scorecard with 5–8 focused items is easier to act on than one with 20 vague criteria. You can always add more items as your team matures.
</Tip>

- **Be specific** — "Did the CSR ask for the customer's name in the first 30 seconds?" is better than "Was the greeting good?"
- **Weight what matters most** — Booking confirmation should probably carry more weight than the exact wording of a greeting
- **Review and iterate** — Run the scorecard for 2–3 weeks, then look at what's consistently failing and adjust

## Applying scorecards to specific call types

You can create separate scorecards for different scenarios:

| Scenario | Recommended scorecard |
|----------|-----------------------|
| Inbound booking calls | Focus on needs assessment, urgency, and closing |
| After-hours calls | Shorter — focus on capturing contact info and booking |
| Cancellation calls | Focus on save attempts and re-scheduling |
| Outbound follow-up | Focus on re-engagement and rebooking |

## Viewing scorecard results

Go to **Call Center AI → Calls** and click any call to see its full scorecard breakdown — which items passed, which failed, and the overall score.
